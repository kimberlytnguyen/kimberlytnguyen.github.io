About Me

It all started when my brother taught me how to Photoshop my 9-year-old face onto the body of an orangutan.

All my life, I've been drawn to making cool stuff digitally. Photoshop was my first love as an early teen. I taught myself the ins and outs of Photoshop, all while learning to create images of my dog riding rainbows in space. Soon after, I got my first DSLR camera and discovered my natural eye for composition, which proved to be useful later when I became a photographer for Humans of UCLA and generated top-performing content. In high school, I was all about video creation and was a leading creator in my broadcast journalism class. I tried to make it big on YouTube - all while using my family's tape camcorder. In college, my entrepreneurial spirit led me to graphic design and I ran a successful side hustle as a top-rated seller creating flyers for small businesses. After college, I taught myself basic HTML/CSS and learned to make this website.

When I'm not gleefully tapping away behind my laptop, you can find me tending to my plant children, learning dance choreography at my favorite studio, or whipping up a tasty dish in the kitchen.
